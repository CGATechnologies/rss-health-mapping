About Service Scoring


The ‘Service’ score and analysis is based on the WHO ‘Service Availability and Readiness Assessment’ (SARA); it is adapted so that it is more applicable to the South Sudan context. We have also taken into consideration the WHO Health Resource Availability Mapping (HeRAMS) as well to ensure that essential elements are covered.


The scoring for overall services for the facility is based on the combined score from the Service Readiness and Service Availability categories.


We calculate a general service readiness score for each facility, and a service-specific score (which combines indicators on both readiness and availability).

General Service Readiness

The Service Readiness category is made up of 5 domains, each with ‘tracer indicators’ that identify the availability of items or capacities. The 5 service domains include:

  1. Basic Amenities
  2. Basic Equipment
  3. Standard Precautions for Infection Prevention
  4. Diagnostic Capacity
  5. Essential Medicines

Under each domain, tracer indicators are assigned a point based on whether they are present/available or not. The overall score represents the total number of items available within the domain.

  1. Basic Amenities
    1. Power/electricity
    2. Improved water source
    3. Room with auditory and visual privacy for patients
    4. Access to adequate sanitation/toilet facility
    5. Communication Equipment
    6. Access to computer and internet

    2. Child scale

    4. Stethoscope

    2. Safe final disposal of infectious waste
    3. Appropriate storage of sharps
    4. Appropriate storage of infectious waste
    5. Disinfectant
    6. Single-use, standard disposable or auto-disable syringes
    7. Soap
    8. Latex gloves
    9. Guidelines for standard precautions
  4. Diagnostic Capacity
    1. Haemoglobin
    2. Blood gluclose
    3. Malaria diagnostic capacity
    4. Urine dipstick - protein
    5. Urine dipstick - glucose
    6. HIV diagnostic capacity
    7. Syphillis RDT

    1. Amlodipine tablet or alternative calcium channel blocker
    2. Amoxicillin (syrup/suspension or dispersible tablets)
    3. Amoxicillin tablet
    4. Ampicillin powder for injection
    5. Aspirin (capsules/tablets)
    6. Beclometasone inhaler
    7. Beta blocker (e.g.bisoprolol, metaprolol, carvedilol, atenolol)
    8. Carbamazepine tablet
    9. Ceftriaxone injection
    10. Diazepam injection
    11. Enalapril tablet or alternative ACE inhibitor (e.g. lisonopril, Ramipril, perindopril)
    12. Fluoxetine tablet
    13. Gentamicin injection
    14. Glibenclamide tablet
    15. Haloperidol tablet
    16. Insulin regular injection
    17. Magnesium sulfate injectable
    18. Metformin tablet
    19. Omeprazole tablet or alternative (e.g. pantoprazole, rabeprazole)
    20. Oral rehydration solution
    21. Oxytocin injection
    22. Salbutamol inhaler
    23. Simvastatin tablet or other statin (e.g. atorvastatin, pravastatin, fluvastatin)
    24. Thiazide (e.g. hydrochlorothiazide)
    25. Zinc sulphate (tablet or syrup)

Service-Specific Availability and Readiness Scores

Service availability and readiness score is based on (at minimum): a. If the facility states that the service is provided, b. If guidelines for the service are available at the facility, and c. If staff at the facility have been trained in the service. For some services, we also include if equipment and diagnostics are available.


The services included in the Service Score include:

  1. Family Planning
  2. Antenatal Care
  3. Delivery Services
  4. Immunisation
  5. Child Health
  6. Adolescent Health
  7. Malaria
  8. TB
  9. HIV
  10. STD
